The Doctor's Many Companions(So Far)

This list contains the names of every companion that has been in more than just one story on the TV series "Doctor Who," as well as their connection to the Doctor and/or what they do for a living, as well as some additional notes about them. Some are listed more than once because they were companions for more than one Doctor. WARNING: May contain spoilers!

The First Doctor(1963-1966)

  1. Susan Foreman(granddaughter)
  2. Barbara Wright and Ian Chesterton(Susan's school teachers)
  3. Vicki(survivor of a crash landing in the future. Was rescued by the Doctor)
  4. Steven Taylor(captured by robots in an alien jungle. The Doctor helped him escape)
  5. Katarina(handmaiden to Cassandra, High Priestess of Troy in 1184 B.C. Helped the injured Steven and got carried away)
  6. Dorthea "Dodo" Chaplet(saw someone injured and went to the TARDIS to call for help)
  7. Polly Wright(secretary who, unknowingly, worked for the bad guys. Joined the party by accident)
  8. Ben Jackson(sailor who met Polly in a nightclub and helped defeat the bad guys)

The Second Doctor(1966-1969)

  1. Polly Wright
  2. Ben Jackson
  3. Jamie McCrimmon(18th century Scotsman. One of the most loyal companions. He appeared in 21 stories with the Doctor)
  4. Victoria Waterfield(daughter of a scientist from 1866, was taken prisoner by the Daleks when her father was killed. Taken in by the Doctor)
  5. Zoe Heriot(a very intelligent girl from a 21st century space station who stowed away in the TARDIS)
  6. Brigadeir Alistar Gordon Lethbridge-Stewart(in charge of the United Nations Intelligence Taskforce(U.N.I.T.))

The Third Doctor(1970-1974)

  1. Elisabeth "Liz" Shaw(U.N.I.T. scientist who was too smart for the Doctor)
  2. Josephine "Jo" Grant(U.N.I.T. scientist/lab assistant who was much more appreciative of the Doctor's abilities)
  3. Sarah Jane Smith(jounalist. One of the most memorable companions)
  4. Brigadier Alistar Gordan Lethbridge-Stewart(appeared off-and-on with many other Doctors)
  5. Sergeant John Benton(serves under the Brigadier, appears with other Doctors with different ranks)
  6. Captain Mike Yates(serves under the Brigadier)

The Fourth Doctor(1974-1981)

  1. Sarah Jane Smith
  2. Harry Sullivan(chief medical officer at U.N.I.T.)
  3. Leela(female warrior from another planet)
  4. K-9(robotic dog from the year 5,000)
  5. Romana(a female Time Lord from the Doctor's home planet of Gallifrey. Her full name is Romanadvoratrelundar. Thank goodness the Doctor shortened it)
  6. Adric(a young math wiz from another planet. Proved his mettle when he made the ultimate sacrifice)
  7. Tegan Jovanka(Australian flight attendant who joined the fun by accident)
  8. Nyssa(humanoid daughter of an alien aristocrat. Good with cybernetics and bioengineering. Taken in by the Doctor after her father was killed)

The Fifth Doctor(1981-1984)

  1. Adric
  2. Tegan Jovanka
  3. Nyssa
  4. Vislor Turlough(alien humanoid exiled to Earth. Tasked with killing the Doctor, but chose not to)
  5. Kamelion(shape-shifting android. A hinderance-turned-companion)
  6. Perpugilliam "Peri" Brown(American botany student)

The Sixth Doctor(1984-1986)

  1. Peri Brown
  2. Melanie "Mel" Bush(was present at the Doctor's trial. Her association with the Doctor is so complex even I cannot comprehend it)

The Seventh Doctor(1986-1989)

  1. Melanie "Mel" Bush
  2. Dorothy Gale "Ace" McShane(expert in chemistry and can easily look out for herself)

The Eighth Doctor(1996)

  1. Grace Holloway(American cardiologist. After the Doctor is shot in San Francisco in 1999, she accidentally kills him when she attempts surgery. He regenerates and they save the world in time to ring in the new year)

The Ninth Doctor(2005)

  1. Rose Tyler(shopgirl. Joined the Doctor after he blew up her job and helped him save the world)
  2. Adam Mitchell(English scientist from 2012. Was in only two episodes)
  3. Captain Jack Harkness(time agent from the 51st century, masquerading as a captain from the American Army who was stationed in London in 1941. Accidentally made immortal)

The Tenth Doctor(2005-2010)

  1. Rose Tyler
  2. Mickey Smith(Rose's (ex)boyfriend)
  3. Martha Jones(medical student. Joined U.N.I.T. soon after leaving the TARDIS)
  4. Donna Noble(temp from Chiswick. First seen in "The Runaway Bride" before becoming a regular companion in "Partners in Crime")
  5. Sarah Jane Smith(returned for "School Reunion" and "The Stolen Earth/Journey's End," as well as a cameo in "The End of Time." Total number of appearances with the Doctor: 23)
  6. Captain Jack Harkness(returned near the end of Series 3 and 4)
  7. Wilfred Mott(Donna's grandfather)

The Eleventh Doctor(2010-2013)

  1. Amelia "Amy" Pond(kiss-o-gram-turned-model-turned-author)
  2. Rory Williams(nurse and husband to Amy)
  3. Professor River Song(archeologist, first seen with the Tenth Doctor. Daughter of Amy and Rory. Married the Doctor at the end of Series 6, making Amy and Rory the Doctor's parents-in-law)
  4. Craig Owens(ordinary guy, only seen in two episodes, one in Series 5, the other in Series 6)
  5. Clara Oswald(first seen as Oswin Oswald at the beginning of Series 7. Seen again as Clara Oswin Oswald in "The Snowmen." These two characters are echoes of the original Clara, whom we meet as a governess in "The Bells of Saint John." All of this complexity is explained in the episode "The Name of the Doctor")

The Twelfth Doctor(2013-present)

  1. Clara Oswald(now works as an English teacher. By the time Clara left the show at the end of Series 9, she had appeared an overall total of 37 times with the Doctor, making her the longest-lasting companion ever on the show. To date)
